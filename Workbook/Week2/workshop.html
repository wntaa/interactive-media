Crazy 8 Design Exercise

The Crazy 8 exercise is a rapid ideation technique where designers sketch eight different ideas in eight minutes. This fast-paced method encourages quick thinking and prevents overthinking, allowing for a wide range of creative concepts to emerge in a short amount of time.

Interactive Analysis: JODI

jodi.org

JODI's website is an experimental web art project that deliberately subverts conventional design principles and user expectations. The site challenges traditional notions of usability and aesthetics by creating an intentionally disorienting and unpredictable browsing experience.

Text and Code

The homepage displays a dense cluster of green ASCII text on a black background, which, upon closer inspection or by viewing the page's source code, reveals a diagram resembling a hydrogen bomb. This approach emphasizes the often-hidden layers of web design, prompting users to engage more deeply with the underlying code.

Hyperlinks

Various elements within the chaotic interface are clickable, leading users to unexpected pages or triggering erratic behaviours. This unpredictability challenges users' expectations of standard hyperlink functionality.

Pop-up Windows

Certain interactions result in a proliferation of pop-up windows that move unpredictably across the screen, creating a sense of disorientation and questioning the stability of the browsing experience.

Animations

The site incorporates strobing patterns and jittery movements, contributing to a sense of instability and challenging traditional notions of web aesthetics.
