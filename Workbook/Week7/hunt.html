Exploring Physical Computing Projects

Find & document (with a reference & one sentence & 1 image) 3 × examples of interesting / engaging / quirky / unique / or otherwise noteworthy PHYSICAL COMPUTING examples. These could be wearables, installations, design or art objects.

How are they interactive? What do they do?

I explored this Behance search for inspiration.

1. MindInGo

What it is: A wearable headband that detects brainwave activity and responds with ambient soundscapes and visual feedback to guide users through meditation or focus sessions.

How it's interactive: Uses EEG sensors to measure the user's mental state and dynamically adjusts audiovisuals to support mindfulness.

What it does: Creates a biofeedback loop — the calmer your mind becomes, the more harmonious the environment becomes, helping users train their brains to relax or concentrate.

“MindInGo is an immersive experience offering a unique perception of yourself.”

2. Oggway – Wooden Box Email Interface

What it is: A wooden box with tactile interaction that lets users manage emails through touch-based sorting.

How it's interactive: Tapping once releases a single email; holding releases all — light and sound signal unread messages.

What it does: Slows down and calms the experience of digital communication using physical, deliberate gestures to filter inboxes.

“Touch when you want the emails let through.”

3. Six-Forty by Four-Eighty – Interactive Pixel Wall

What it is: A massive wall of 220 magnetic pixels that respond to touch and motion like a physical screen.

How it's interactive: Users touch the surface to change colours, trigger movement, or use a remote control to shift animations in speed or sharpness.

What it does: Makes digital pixels tangible — transforming an entire wall into a living, responsive visual space.
